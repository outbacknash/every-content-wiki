# Writing Guardrails
Type: Concept | Category: Editorial Workflow

A system for using AI to audit AI-generated content and maintain a human-like voice.

## The Problem
Drafts written with AI often pick up "AI tells":
- Symmetrical sentence structures.
- "Rhetorical throat-clears" (introductory filler).
- Phrases that sound profound but are empty.
- Repetitive "pesky sets of three."

## The /guardrails Skill
A "skill" (instructions for an AI agent) that acts as an exacting editorial specialist. 
- **Personas:** Using specialized reviewers like "Sorkin" (momentum), "Mom" (accessibility), and "Asshole" (rigorous logic).
- **Function:** Catches weak spots before they reach a human editor.

## The Human Role
Using guardrails doesn't reduce work; it shifts it. The human must define clear standards so the model can enforce them, which in turn helps the human understand their own writing preferences better.
