# Writing Guardrails

A system for using AI to audit AI-generated content and maintain a human-like voice.

## Definition
Writing Guardrails is an editorial methodology where specialized AI agents (skills) are used to detect and strip out "AI tells"—common linguistic patterns and stylistic artifacts introduced by large language models.

## How It Works
The system uses specialized reviewer personas or a `/guardrails` skill:
- **Detection:** Flags symmetrical sentence structures, "rhetorical throat-clears," and repetitive "pesky sets of three."
- **Personas:** Employs characters like "Sorkin" for momentum or "Asshole" for logic to critique the text.
- **Iteration:** The human defines these standards in documentation, allowing agents to enforce them autonomously.

## Business Impact
Guardrails shift the human workload from drafting to standard-setting and auditing. This ensures high-quality, authentic-sounding content while still benefiting from the speed of AI generation, preventing the "machine smoothness" that can alienate readers.
