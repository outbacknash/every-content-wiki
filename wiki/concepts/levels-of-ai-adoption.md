# Levels of AI Adoption

A framework developed by Mike Taylor (building on Steve Yegge's work) to map the stages of AI integration in personal and professional workflows.

## Definition
The Levels of AI Adoption is a taxonomic framework that classifies how deeply an individual or organization has integrated AI into their operations, ranging from simple information retrieval (Level 1) to full project delegation and autonomous loops (Level 8).

## How It Works
The framework uses specific behaviors and "signals" to determine current level:
- **Level 1:** Basic Chatbot (simple Q&A).
- **Level 7/8:** Orchestrator/Delegator. Users delegate entire projects, allow agents to work asynchronously, review results, and feed learnings back into the next iteration.
- **Transition Signal:** The shift from "Prompts" (single-turn) to "Loops" (iterative/autonomous).

## Business Impact
Determining the correct level of adoption allows organizations to balance the speed of AI output against the cost of potential errors and the level of human trust required. It shifts the focus from tool selection to workflow architecture.
