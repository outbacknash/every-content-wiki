# The Eight Levels of AI Adoption

A framework by Mike Taylor and Laura Entis (Every.to) for assessing and increasing AI usage within organizations, mapping the progression from simple chatbot interactions to fully autonomous systems.

## The Taxonomy

| Level | Name | Description | Example Tools |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Chatbot** | Basic task-based interaction. You give a prompt, it provides a response. | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini |
| 2 | **Copilot** | AI integrated into existing workflows to assist with specific sub-tasks in real-time. | GitHub Copilot, Microsoft 365 Copilot |
| 3 | **Custom Bot** | Purpose-built bots with specific instructions or knowledge (RAG) for a single domain. | Custom GPTs, Claude Projects |
| 4 | **Agent** | AI that can use tools and execute multi-step plans with minimal human intervention. | AutoGPT, BabyAGI, Spiral |
| 5 | **Workflow** | Chained AI steps where the output of one model serves as the input for the next. | Zapier Central, Make.com, LangChain |
| 6 | **Team** | Multiple specialized agents collaborating on a complex, shared objective. | Multi-agent systems, CrewAI, AutoGen |
| 7 | **System** | AI deeply integrated into core business logic and databases, driving decision-making. | AI-native enterprise systems |
| 8 | **Autonomous Organization** | The organization's core value proposition is delivered primarily by autonomous AI systems. | Future AI-native entities |

## How It Works
Organizations typically move through these levels linearly as they build trust and infrastructure. Each level represents an increase in both technical complexity and the degree of delegation from human to machine.
